I started this puzzle theme with a Monday-ish approach. Gathering up phrases like OVERSIZEBAGGAGE, FAMILYDRAMA, TOKYOSUBWAY, etc. All things that tend to be "a lot to unpack." But I couldn't quite land a theme set I liked.

Eventually the idea of "unpacking" a rebus sprang to mind. I really liked the concept of unstuffing a rebus from its square, one letter at a time.

Starting with the revealer at the bottom, I continued the theme building upward with ALOE VERA LOTION and CALIFORNIA LOTTO (beating FLORIDA LOTTO for symmetry). And noticed the circled squares were rising up the grid in a pleasing pattern. So to keep with that pattern, I needed a topmost themer that ended with "ALOT." ANDTHATSSAYINGALOT worked out nicely, I think.

Apologies to SIRMIXALOT… I tried!

Hope you enjoyed the puzzle!

Jeremy is one of my favorite constructors regarding ideas that climb the ranks. AND THAT'S SAYING A LOT, considering how many constructors I admire!

What a fun idea to expand (ALOT), using the justification of IT'S A LOT TO UNPACK. Such an orderly progression, too, from ALOT to ALO T to AL O T to A L O T. Very different from my and my kids' unpacking method, which would see A L O T immediately chucked to the four winds, much to my wife's chagrin.

Tricksy Thursdays can be frustrating for some solvers, so a handful of cluing delights go a long way to keeping up interest. I laughed at JAPAN as the "homeland" for Mothra and Gamera. [Big band staple] piqued the interest of this former jazz band member. (A TUBA is literally a big instrument in a band.) I also enjoyed thinking about a big wheel captain steering the HELM's big wheel.

And those were only some of the TOP TEN! [A big (music) hit might break it] is a creative way to play on TOP TEN.

Great gridwork — I'd expect nothing less from Jeremy. Perfect layout, taking advantage of the 13-letter themers to run MADE A CAMEO and TECHNO BAND through their ends. Weaving in IN HEELS / NAIL SALONS / PIE PLATE / VIETNAM, all at the low, low price of ON UP and ELO (Electric Light Orchestra)? Jeremy's years of hard work shows through brilliantly.

Ultimately, the rebus to non-rebus progression wasn't enough to wow me — too orderly for my slovenly habits — but there was a lot to unpack and appreciate throughout the solve.
