Jeff Chen notes:
DOOMSCROLLING / CANCEL CULTURE / WHITE RUSSIANS … nothing controversial to see here! What a story that trio of marquee answers tells; ... read more

DOOMSCROLLING / CANCEL CULTURE / WHITE RUSSIANS … nothing controversial to see here! What a story that trio of marquee answers tells; a tale for our time. The grid art enhances the picture, too, with a cyclone storming around. Not to mention KIEV and LAZARUS.

I've loved the word PLOTZ ever since a Jewish friend of mine used it. There's something so fun about Yiddishisms — he's introduced me to many over a bagel and SCHMEAR.

This "pinwheel" layout lends itself so well to ease of filling, allowing each corner to be tackled separately. Once the triple-stair-stack of marquee answers are in place, the lower left and upper right can be filled independently.

Some editors are particular about the number of short entries in a themeless, especially 3-letter ones. It's rare to see one with this many (17). My solve felt more start-and-stop than usual, and that might have contributed.

This contract bridge fanatic loved seeing ENDPLAY, although it wasn't as much of an EGO BOOST because I confidently filled in FINESSE at first. Seems appropriate to not finesse that answer — after a decade of playing, I still have trouble stripping suits to prepare for the devious ENDPLAY.

Get your mind out of the gutter.

Some great entries around the perimeter — STONEWALL as the historic monument and BOOK FAIRS reading this bibliophile's preferences so well — helped me overcome some of the DOOM and gloom that the center triplet and grid art evoked.
