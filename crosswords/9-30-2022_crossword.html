This crossword started with me trying to see how many interesting 15-letter spanners I could fit into a grid — without cramming them all into stacks, and while trying to keep the rest of the fill reasonably fun and engaging.

My favourite entry is 20-Across, with 42-Across being a close second. I was pleased that my original clues survived the editing process in both cases.

I started making crosswords as a pandemic hobby, learning through trial and mostly error. After 21 rejections from the Times, I had almost abandoned hope of getting a "yes" from Will and the editing team. It seems fitting that their 22nd response was a "yes", as 22 is my lucky number (I like twos, and 22 is even better as it is two twos).

It's been a while since we've had a "tic-tac-toe" themeless. These usually live or die on the snazziness of the six grid-spanning entries forming the tic-tac-toe backbone, along with the quality of their clues. I used to love the SLAM DUNK CONTEST — 2016 Zach Lavine / Aaron Gordon duel! It's since fallen apart, the latest one depressing. Not one of the NBA's SEXIEST MAN ALIVE candidates participated.

Some of the others almost tell a story. HIGH STAKES POKER, with COLLECTORS ITEMS in the pot … I don't know what the MORAL OF THE STORY is, but I'd love to find out!

There's no doubt that the jarabe tapatio is the national dance of Mexico. I get flashbacks of my brother's kid — the only Asian in his elementary school class — being asked to dress up in a dragon costume. I'm sure this says a lot more about me and my hang-ups than anything.

A tic-tac-toe themeless usually has room for two or possibly four more long entries, and NAP TIMES and ESCAPE KEY are assets. PLACATED is a single-worder that might not placate editors, and HENS TEETH has an old-timey feel to it.

Tic-tac-toes tend to be incredibly difficult to fill cleanly, since the rigid skeleton constrains each and every subregion. David did well to escape with AON AVS FLOR HOC, with HOC being super minor; only dinged because it's hard to clue without reusing [Ad ___]. And I'm sure Colorado Avalanche fans will call me for icing.

Solid solve, bolstered by a sprinking of great clues. I appreciated the wordplay in that NW corner — ALEXA on Amazon Echo disguised as [Echo voice], plus People magazine's capital P similarly hidden in [People person?] made for a fantastic pair of "hidden capitalizations."
