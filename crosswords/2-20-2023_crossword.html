I never like having to resort to big "Utah" black squares in a puzzle, but the lengths of the theme entries just wouldn't cooperate with another arrangement. Hopefully, TINDER MATCH is a fun enough marquee entry to justify them.

For Downs-only solvers, I hope you enjoy the 39- to 44-Down succession of a HOT TODDY and SNYDERS pretzels followed by a CAPFUL of Pepto-Bismol. Sounds like a good way to celebrate the long weekend to me.

Jeff Chen notes:
It seems apt that JUICE, PRESS, and SQUEEZE can all precede the word BOX. I swear upon all that is holy that I will wreak my revenge ... read more

It seems apt that JUICE, PRESS, and SQUEEZE can all precede the word BOX. I swear upon all that is holy that I will wreak my revenge on the inventor of the JUICE BOX. If I had a nickel every time my kids pressed and squeezed one to explode a food-colored, sugar-loaded fountain of disaster …

Time to get off my SOAPBOX (and get the SOAP to clean up the mess).

As far as "both words can precede" themes go, this one is solid, avoiding the usual knocks on this genre:

  • All themer halves are recognizable as types of BOXES. Perhaps people who aren't fans of The Who or accordions might not click with SQUEEZEBOX, but it's legit.
  • The themers are colorful. I can often tell when this genre is coming since the themers tend to be dry at best. Not today.
  • Excellent revealer. FUSE two types of BOXES together = spot-on.

Solvers antsy with this genre will appreciate sitting back with a HOT TODDY. ANTSIEST isn't the most soothing of bonuses, but I enjoyed much of DENZEL, BLUE JAY, POODLES.

Working with a central 11 is so rough — it often creates situations where Down fill has to work through three themers. PESETA might be tough for newbs, and DOES SO does so little.

AREOLAS would be much more familiar if the NYT were less uptight.

A long time ago, a friend and I tried to take "both words can precede X" to the next level, but it was received with mixed reactions. I'd love to see different ideas that might help breathe more life into this well-worn genre.
