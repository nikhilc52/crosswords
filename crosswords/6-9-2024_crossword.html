Zachary Schiff is a writer living in New York City. This is his first crossword for The Times.

The theme was initially planned for a 15x15 weekday puzzle, but it didn't quite pan out — through "trial and error and a little bit of luck," he was able to rework it into a Sunday. His favorite clue is 89-Down, a fact he heard a few years back and stored away in the hopes of one day including in a puzzle.

After too many rejections to count, I am finally making my NY Times debut (though I have had puzzles published elsewhere).

For a couple of years, I had wanted to do a "SILENT __" puzzle, and my first idea was SILENT NIGHT as a 15x15 weekday puzzle, but 5 themers and a revealer proved to be too messy. I switched to SILENT FILM, but after a lot of tries, I couldn't come up with a grid I liked. Eventually, I decided to go the Sunday route, which is much, much harder to do and so much more time-consuming, but when I settled on SILENT AUCTION, it all fell together. Of course, it still took 30+ hours and two revisions to create, but I'm happy with the results.

They kept a lot of my original clues, which I was happy about. I'm jazzed that they kept my hockey and shuffleboard clues intact as well as my trivia about the cost of the 1970 NYC marathon and the Schwarzenegger-Pratt connection.

Of course, there are some entries I couldn't avoid (OAS, RATA and NOAIR are the ones I'm thinking of), but to get a Sunday that you're happy with, concessions have to be made.
