I had GOOOOOOOOOOOOAL in my list of potential themeless seeds, but pivoted after noticing WORLDCUP and VUVUZELA are the same length. You're less likely to hear one now, though, as they were banned in 2014. Hope people enjoy!

Like many Americans, soccer hasn't captured my attention as much as other sports, but I've recently been fascinated by Welcome to Wrexham. Now, I want to sit in the Kop and cheer on Paul Mullin and Ollie Palmer — in a Deadpool costume, of course. It's fun to see my ex-pat friends catch WORLD CUP fever, rooting on their home country teams.

At first, the OLE x5 repetition felt like a cheap way to create a 15-letter grid spanner, but then I realized there's a real chant that uses exactly five OLEs. Neat!

The excessive Os in the next themer also got my OO (eyes) rolling. YouTube to the rescue again! There's something so endearing about announcers' enthusiastic elongations that I'm OOOOOOOOOOOOO-K with it.

VUVUZELA — banned in many venues for over a decade, thankfully — is a tough word to spell correctly, and expecting newer solvers to figure out the ZELLE crossing is a Zinedine Zidane head-butt. (SMAZE crossing LLANO is more like a cheap slide tackle.)

There's nothing like some personality injected into an early-week clue. FLAW is a common entry, and I'm crying GOOOOOOOOOOOOAL with this perfect shot: [Having a thermal exhaust port lead straight to the reactor of your Death Star, e.g.].

The SW and NE corners are so wide-open — even more "go big or go home" than pulling your goalie. There can be some incredible bonuses, like RUN WILD and THIS WON'T DO, with EYE CONTACT and GOOD ONE. However, OATERS STYES and Kevin NEALON make me so thankful that Michael Che and Colin Jost have freshened up "Weekend Update."

Although there were some offsides penalties and yellow cards, I enjoyed the celebratory feel of today's puzzle, foreshadowing all the excitement ahead in the upcoming weeks.
