David Rockow notes:

Look at me crossing SIENA/RENI on a Monday. I am a bit of a bad boy if I do say so myself.

Jeff Chen notes:

In her retirement, my mom has taken an affinity to Taiwanese black bears. This would be all well and good, but she's what's known as a "roundabout storyteller." There have been many nights filled with tales of how the "moon bear" got its distinctive markings, leading to how her dear friend attempted to trek to Hualien but then got a call from Mr. Tsai at the zoo, and then the foundation insisted on accompanying Ms. Chau, who she went to school with …

And you wonder where I get it from. BEAR WITH ME, as usual.

Great phrase to play on, and a couple of excellent findings. Hard to believe that JAMES BROWN makes his NYT crossword debut today! MEN IN BLACK is one of my favorite sci-fi classics, and my daughter is obsessed with POLAROID CAMERAs.

Although I enjoyed MOMENT IN THE SUN as a colorful phrase, I paused ... is a SUN BEAR a thing? It absolutely is, though. For the love of all that is holy, please don't tell my mom.

Three MEs to the left, one to the right. Three types of BEAR that I immediately recognized, one that I didn't. Three full bears and one hidden in POLAROID. Was there a way to even things out?

Best I could come up with was using an EXPANDABLE MESH stent to open up some balance. Then I got a call from Jim Horne, or was it my dentist who asked if I could come in a hour earlier to talk about my cousin, Mr. Yu …

Ahem. Fresh, interesting theme that wasn't perfectly balanced but used reasonable trade-offs. I also appreciated getting some refreshing LEMON LIME in my SEVEN SEAS to help offset some of the MOUE-inducing SMEE.
