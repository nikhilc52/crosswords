Sam Ezersky notes:

Sometimes, a single terrible pun — anchored at 1-Across — is more than enough to inspire an entire themeless puzzle.

Jeff Chen notes:
I always brace myself when I see Sam's byline, given his penchant for skewing waaaaay hard, even on the Saturday scale. What a joy to ... read more

I always brace myself when I see Sam's byline, given his penchant for skewing waaaaay hard, even on the Saturday scale. What a joy to not only be able to finish today's, but to not get utterly frustrated anywhere!

Fantastic anchor entry in ARE YOU HIGH?! It's hard to imagine the NYT running this ten years ago, and I love the change. I would say I GOT NOTHIN' when it comes to other entries I loved, but MEMPHIS BBQ tickles the taste buds, the FILE SERVER / SPORTS DESK serves up color commentary, PGA COURSE shoots under par, and CAMEL HUMPS is a great way to kick things off.

SPORTSDESK might not be that fun for sportsball haters, but that clue made it so appealing. I play a lot of board games, so [Purveyor of game pieces] had me thinking about my local toy shop. Great wordplay here, "pieces" hinting at pieces of games that sportscasters recap.

I did hesitate on a couple entries — fewer than usual for one of Sam's puzzles, though. SHREWISH made me wince, as it felt like an amped-up HAG with its potential to offend. QUITE BAD wasn't quite good, but neither was it quite terrible. Finally, LEADOFFS is a strange plural, much more commonly leadoff hitters.

Overall though, a ton of great material that far overcame my hesitations.
