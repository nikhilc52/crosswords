Kristian House notes:
I always have a list of words and phrases that I would like to get into a crossword puzzle, so I decided to plunk a few of those into ... read more

I always have a list of words and phrases that I would like to get into a crossword puzzle, so I decided to plunk a few of those into a grid and work around them to see what came out. This puzzle is the result of that experiment. A few of my favorites are GLAMPING, CORNHOLE, RAGE QUIT, and NERD CRED. I'm especially happy that I could cross RAPINOE and LUCINDA in the center because Megan Rapinoe and Lucinda Williams are both amazing at what they do.

Will changed a few of my original clues to make the puzzle a little more challenging than when I originally wrote it, but I'm glad he kept my clue for CHEERIOS. I love nerdy trivia like that. I hope everyone enjoyed the puzzle!

I'm a chemistry buff, and I knew with 100% confidence — no purity assay required! — that [Atoms that have the same number of neutrons] couldn't be ISOTOPES, which have the same number of protons. Yet, I still wrote in ISOTOPES. What else could ISOTO_ES be, after all?

D'oh!

So I was already hesitant when I filled in PURGATIO_. What else could that be? Fool me twice, shame on me! Could it be ... PURGATIOL? PURGATIOS?

This fool should have recognized the similarity to "purgatory," where I'm clearly headed after this shameful post. So much for my NERD CRED.

Thankfully, there was enough great DUCT TAPE (which I've used to construct load-bearing walls in my daughter's dollhouses), CORNHOLE (surprisingly fun!), ROAD BIKE with a great clue about not being for trails.

And that clue for BRACKET! People who don't follow sports might not get it — think about March Madness brackets, where people have to pick from 64 different seeded teams. "Collection of seeds" is deviously innocent wordplay.

A 72-word (the max allowable for a themeless) needs to squeeze every last drop of potential out of every long slot, so entries like EXTERNAL and GYMNASIA felt like something got left on the table.

I enjoyed the solve overall, especially after coming to terms with the word ISOTONES. With a non-audacious grid design, though, it's tough to overlook some weirdness.
