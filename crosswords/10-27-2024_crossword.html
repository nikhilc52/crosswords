Jeffrey Martinovic is a mechanical engineering student at Western University in London, Ontario. Jeff Chen, of Seattle, is a writer and longtime Times crossword contributor.

They brainstormed puzzle ideas and entries over email, and nearly three months, 200 messages and 100 grid versions later, submitted the artful puzzle here. The theme is neatly explained at 111-Across.

Constructor notes:

Jeffrey: The basic idea for this puzzle (fittingly) came to me during a meeting last summer, while I was working for a professor in my school's astronomy department. Before long, Jeff and I had brainstormed around the concept enough to begin filling up a grid. That's where the real fun began - it took dozens (maybe even hundreds) of iterations between us to create the grid you see before you. I always enjoy working with Jeff on puzzles because he is super creative, but also rigorous in his approach, especially when it comes to constructing grids. This puzzle in particular was a test of determination due to its highly constrained layout and visual elements. Hopefully you enjoyed solving, and happy Sunday!

I'll end on a couple fun facts about the moon: The moon is tidally locked with the earth, meaning we always see the same side of it. Also, the moon is very slowly drifting away from the earth at a rate of a couple inches each year!
