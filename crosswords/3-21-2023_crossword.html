Repeat after me … again! Again!!!

TABOOBOOBOO looks like a BOO BOO, but it sure is fun to say.

This concept has been around for a long time, but I hadn't seen any of these specific themers. QATAR TARTAR made me laugh with its sheer ridiculousness, and although I couldn't remember the word for a Russian villa, the theme helped me piece together DACHA.

The "repeated syllables" genre is tricky. Old hands will have seen variants of it so many times, that after the first themer, they can jump down and fill in the other themers right away. Newer solvers may not have seen it though, and let me tell you, there's not much more intriguing than trying to make out what's said during a LEMUR MURMUR.

Just as long as these cute little animals aren't plotting something most foul in their whisperings — no LEMUR MURMUR MURDERs for me, thank you very much.

I usually favor multi-word bonuses for their color, but DECADENT is a decadent one-worder. Broccoli is a staple of my growing plant-based diet, and it's not every day that you see the word FLORETS in a crossword.

After yesterday's [What did the fish say …] = DAM, we get the acorn saying "Gee, I'm a tree!" I almost reflexively made an excuse to go into the other room to get away from my joke-telling kids, but it's never too early to get the next generation hooked into the crossworld.

It's tough to emerge from the deep well of repeated-syllables puzzles, but Michael's efforts made me smile.
