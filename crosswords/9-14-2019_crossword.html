I like finding 15-letter entries that haven't been used before. This puzzle has five grid-spanners, all making their Shortz-era NYT debut.

These two were my seed entries: VIRTUE SIGNALING (which has been going on since the beginning of time — now we have a name for it) and PIZZA DELIVERIES (I just really liked my clue).

Figuring out the starts of the two marquee across entries was such a treat. I blanked on ___ HOME RUN for the longest time, scouring my mental archives for types of homers. Walk off? Inside the park? Great moment of relief to finally land on PINCH HIT HOME RUN.

Although, I'm still furious with Kirk Gibson, even 30 years later. FIST PUMP ALL OVER MY A'S, SERIOUSLY? WELL JUST REMEMBER, KARMA'S A BITCH YOU FRIGGIN' (censored).

Not that I'm bitter. What does my therapist know, anyway?

The second horizontal grid-spanner was so much fun, as I was stuck on ___ DELIVERIES for five full minutes. My deficiencies in golf are only overshadowed by my atrocious knowledge of golf terminology, so I could only think of OPEN DELIVERIES. Brilliant a-ha when I realized "slices" meant PIZZA slices. The clue's grammar is as tortured as my golf swing, but heck, it was worth it.

Great vertical backbone connecting the two feature entries, DETAILS TO FOLLOW.

I wasn't as hot on SENSATIONALIZE, as it's a one-worder that feels pedestrian. I begrudgingly read up on VIRTUE SIGNALING because I supposed I should, but it frustrated me during my solve, as I struggled to piece it together, letter by letter.

Breaking up these last two vertical grid-spanners would have given a better overall result, since such a massive and rigid skeleton places too much constraint on a grid. In the SE, for example, BOOTLEG is good, but fewer constraints could have eliminated something no one likes, EDATE.

If only Siri did crosswords.
