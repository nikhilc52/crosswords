I'm usually not that fond of themes with lists of proper names, but this revealer was just sitting there practically begging to be made into a puzzle. Apologies to Fox Mulder and Bobcat Goldthwait for the snubs — you could've gone in the center if the puzzle had a title instead. Hope y'all enjoy!

Old school throwback to the BEASTIE BOYS! After 40 years (!), these songs still hold up.

Okay, maybe not. I sabotaged my credibility yet again.

Nice riff on BEASTIE BOYS, featuring four "boys" with animal first names. WOLF BLITZER is such an awesome name, played upon many times not only for his first name but his last as well. BEAR GRYLLS is even better, so apt for a survival specialist. Don't get up in his grill, bears!

It's a shame about TIGER WOODS — hard to imagine now that most pundits gave him strong odds to take the "most Majors wins" title from Jack Nicklaus.

Mid-week grids often benefit from extra juice in the fill. Brandon could have raised his word count by breaking up some long slots, but the bonuses in BOGEYED (crossing TIGER WOODS, talk about throwing shade!), GO VIRAL, WIENERS, SENATOR, HATED IT hardly made me hate it.

Some tough prices to accept, though, with ATA EXT LBS PIS RET UKE YEE dabbing on the glue. It's not a set of trade-offs I would have made, but I can see the benefits of all those fill extras to help entertain solvers who weren't into the theme.

In the current years-long stretch of male-dominated crosswords, it felt strange to feature a revealer and theme set so heavily weighted that way. Still, the BEASTIE BOYS throwback let me relive some fun memories from my (embarrassing) youth.
