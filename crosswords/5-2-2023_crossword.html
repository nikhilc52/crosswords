I'd been trying to figure out a way to make a puzzle about "shaken martinis" for a while and I'm really glad that I finally figured it out. Also, longtime solvers of my puzzles may notice that this one is a bit more trivia-forward than my crosswords have tended to be in the past. But that's definitely been happening more and more in my work lately, both intentionally and unintentionally.

Trivia became an obsession of mine during the pandemic, and I've been noticing it seeping into my work in new and often surprising (at least to me!) ways. I've included names I would never have thought cross-worthy before, and even sometimes centering themes around "cool facts." It's been fun for me to watch how one facet of my work impacts another as time passes, so I hope the solvers at home are also finding it fun!

I loved when Daniel Craig shook up the world of James Bond. Shaken or stirred? Damn straight, Bond shouldn't care about those things!

I appreciate that Aimee used JAMES BOND as a revealer rather than the much dryer MARTINI. Making that mental leap from Bond to his drink of choice is fun.

Anagrams are ultra-common in cryptic crosswords. Shaken and stirred are both used frequently. Before Craig came along, I'd have been confused by this theme since every other Bond requested shaken, not stirred, which doesn't make much cryptic sense. Yet another reason why Craig is my favorite of the Bonds!

At least until the producers sign on Idris Elba or John Cho.

Finding embedded anagram strings isn't as easy as finding fixed strings of letters, but some hacked-up code does the trick. It would have been so Bondesque if the ASTON MARTIN worked, but it doesn't have that second I, and MARTIN hardly disguises the game. The only other pizzazzy possibilities I found were HENRI MATISSE and SPIRIT ANIMAL, but these 12s would make grid construction challenging.

This Seattleite loves the inclusion of beautiful MT RAINIER, anyway.

I wouldn't say I liked the outdated ALERO up top or AMAIN sailing in the east, but working with a central 9 makes gridding tough. I did enjoy Aimee's balancing act, working in freshies like GANJA and ULTIMATE.

Loved the [It's full of beans] clue for BURRITO, too, plus BURRITO / BURRATA feels like the seed of some TOMAYTO TOMAHTO mantra.

Anagrams are a tough sell these days, but the indirect revealer of JAMES BOND sure was fun.
