Frank Longo, of Hoboken, N.J., is a professional puzzlemaker and editor. He creates the weekly "Premier Crossword" for King Features Syndicate, as well as the Sunday "Spelling Bee" for [the Sunday New York Times] magazine. He is the author of more than 150 books of Sudoku puzzles.

Today's crossword is unthemed, which means the focus is on vocabulary that is as lively and colorful as possible, with no other constraint. To construct it, Frank started with the central Across stack, then built outward. The finished puzzle has 122 answers, the lowest number ever for a Sunday Times crossword.

OPEN WIDE! I wonder how many questions I'll get today, asking what the title means. Clearly, it's a dentist theme. What, you didn't get that? YOU ANTI-DENTITE!

No, no, there is no theme. The title might be too insidery for general consumption, hinting at the fact that this is one of the most wide-open Sunday grids in NYT history, setting a new record for fewest words. It's basically impossible to go down to 122 words in a themed puzzle, but the door cracks open if you decide to go the themeless route.

This is the third themeless Sunday the NYT has done, the first one about a year ago. Back then, I was adamantly against this notion, adhering to the position that it's a defeatist attitude, an admission that the NYT is struggling to come up with captivating Sunday themes. I still believe that, but perhaps I've softened. I did enjoy a lot about this one.

A couple of great long answers, all rarely seen in crosswords: DANIEL CRAIG / COLLEGE PREP, PRISON ESCAPE, BRAND NAME PRODUCTS, TOOK TO THE SLOPES, AMAZON PRIME. That's the kind of stuff that makes solvers sit up.

Some liabilities have to come along with such an audacious effort, though. Kicking off the puzzle with DUCA crossing AFLY was like swallowing a fly. I appreciated Frank's strong efforts to keep the rest of the puzzle impressively smooth, but I did notice two areas in which there were concentrations:

  1. Tough foreign words. CADGING, TROYES, AMAH, SAAR, DESERET. Okay, that last one isn't foreign, but it sounds like it is. And CADGING is maybe more an oddball than foreign. Maybe two or three of these would have bene fine. Five, not so much.
  2. SHORT OF, HINT AT, ALERTED TO, ETCH IN. These are all reasonable entries. Such potential left on the table, though; all of these much more neutral than positive.

I suppose I wouldn't mind one or maybe two of these Sunday themelesses a year, but I'd much rather have Will Shortz and his team focus on coming up with better and more creative ways of spurring constructors on to come up with more Sunday-worthy concepts. Increasing the pay is fantastic, of course, but it's clearly not enough, since Sundays overall have been disappointing this year.

I'd love to see a crack team assembled to tackle the problem head-on. Just imagine what could happen if you tasked the ten most creative minds to deliver ten awesome concepts per year. The Avengers and the Justice League would pale in comparison to the NYT Sunday Squad.
