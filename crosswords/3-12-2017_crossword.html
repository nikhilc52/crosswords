TAKING THE FIFTH interpreted as "add the fifth letter of the alphabet, E, to base phrases, with kooky results." I enjoyed a good number of them: HOT CROSS BUNNY gave a funny image. OFF THE MARQUEE had a great spelling change (MARK to MARQUEE), as did TABLE TENNESSEE (TENNIS to TENNESSEE). ATTILA THE HONEY flips Attila amusingly on his head.

Sound change puzzles often don't do much for me, but I enjoyed today's results as a whole. Not every Sunday puzzle has to be (or should be) groundbreaking, and as a solver, I welcome sharp examples of tried and true theme types.

A couple of nice bonuses in the fill, TIDY SUM, AQUALUNG, FIRE AWAY, PRO TEAM. These good mid-to-long entries helped keep my attention.

I wasn't as hot on the short fill, though. It felt like everywhere in the puzzle, I got bogged down by an abundance of crossword glue. I stopped counting at about ten — ERNO, GTS, ADEE, ISTH, LEU, DEO, MST, and so on — and I still had half the puzzle to go. Way too much; dragged down the quality of my solving experience.

I admire that Alan strives to do more, consistently dipping under 140 words in his Sunday puzzles (this one is at 136). But it's so hard to do while retaining smooth fill. I don't feel like it's worth it, if you have to use so much crossword glue.

Usually, I don't mind if there are bits of glue dabbed over the puzzle — and Alan does well not to concentrate it in any one spot — but there's just so much in total that it felt like it should have been reworked for a smoother overall feel. Going up to 140 words would undoubtedly have helped.

Certain ones felt particularly egregious, too — the Maleskan ITER and ADOLF. Hard to save that last one with any clue.

It's rare that a sound change puzzle tickles my fancy, though, so I did appreciate the good quality and quantity of themers.
