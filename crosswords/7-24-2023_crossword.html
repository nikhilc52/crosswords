The XWord Info Finder was very helpful in searching for entries for this puzzle, as you can just bung in [SCARLET] multiple times. Appropriate for John Ewbank to create a "letter bank" puzzle!

Alas, no U?

Ew.

"Letter banks" are difficult for some newer solvers to grasp. Note that CATTLE CARS, SALT CELLAR, STEER CLEAR, and ART CLASSES are all formed from the set [A C E L R S T].

Thankfully, John developed more interest (get it? bank? interest? okay, fine, it wasn't funny) through another constraint that I didn't notice until much later. Not only do these four phrases draw from the [A C E L R S T] bank, but they all use every one of the letters at least once.

I was all set to muse over why John didn't use more colorful entries like ALL STAR CAST, STATE SECRET, TASTE TESTER, and TEASER RATES (appropriate for a bank puzzle!), when I noticed that each of them lacked at least one of the seven letters. This one goes out to all the Spelling Bee pangram fanatics!

I wonder how many solvers will notice the extra layer. I wouldn't have if I hadn't shifted into analysis mode.

(Emphasis on anal.)

Fantastic gridwork; so many bonuses with zero gluey bits. John had to deploy so many of his black squares around the middle that he was forced to leave big, open corners in the SW and NE. Thankfully, the [A C E L R S T] are easy letters to grid around, and John did his DARNEDEST to juice up the joint with not only doubles like GOOD LOSER and NEWS ALERT, but additional touches like TIP JARS and IM ALL IN. Yes, he is!

It's tough for me to get excited about letter bank concepts these days, but I appreciated John's effort to make his stand out.
