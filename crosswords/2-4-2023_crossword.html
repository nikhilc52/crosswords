This puzzle was my first themeless puzzle to be accepted for publication. I submitted it (by mail!) with a note cautioning that "while the grid may look a bit more like that of a M-Th puzzle, I hope you'll see beyond that..." The fill that delighted me nearly three years ago still delights me today — I'm particularly fond of 19D, 4D, and 45A and their clues.

In the intervening years, I've moved across the country, and I've started learning the accordion (see the glimmers of an idea in my cluing for 7D). Sadly, though, Janelle [26D] hasn't yet received her EGOT. Thanks to the editorial team for keeping both Booty clues and my shoutout to my mom in the final clue of the puzzle!

HITS A NERVE crossing GO OUTSIDE — Katie somehow knows my kids? My MOONSHOT is for them to stop attempting to hit each other's nerves for a full day. I'd settle for an hour.

Let's CUT TO THE CHASE, some delights in the grid. I love a good BEACH READ, and that clue for LETS SEE SOME ID is GORGEOUS indeed. No telltale question mark needed for [Line at the door of a bar]. That's a spoken line, not a queue. I've found TRUE LOVE with that level of clever wordplay!

Some of the long entries were more neutral, though — BLOTCHED blotched all the potential of that slot. BENEVOLENT is a fine and admirable word, but editors usually ask to steer clear of one-worders in favor of colorful multi-worders like STEER CLEAR. Perhaps with a fantastic clue, it could have stood out.

"Clue echoes" can be witty, especially when the same clue is used with different meanings. [Booty] can be a pirate's plundered HAUL, or it can be the butt of a joke, as in one's SEAT. Awesome lateral thinking! I'm not as impressed with repetitive echoes, like ["Be that as it may …"] for both HOWEVER and EVEN SO.

Janelle MONAE is more than just a singer! I have her book on my bedside table. Amazing range of talent.

What a nice save for AERO (usually clued as a prefix). Although the candy bar is more commonly seen in Britain, the "Irresitibubble" tagline makes it so bubbly.
