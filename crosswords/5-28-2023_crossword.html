Interesting take on the letter addition / removal genre. Focusing on a single-letter change is usually a losing battle, since the genre has been run nearly out of town over the decades, so combining things — a letter addition plus a letter removal in the same row — can be productive. I appreciated Chandi and Taylor's extra layer, not using just any combination of U removals + U additions, but riffing on the same word pair, like SIT / SUIT.

CAN I STEAL YOU FOR A SECOND is a colorful, colloquial revealer, too. If all the Us had been stolen out of the second position (as in SUIT but not CAUSE), that would have been yet another layer to help the concept stand out.

U-HAUL is a workable title. Feels like there's something better out there, but I couldn't come up with anything sharper. That's often the problem with trying to integrate both a clever revealer and title — one will be amazing while the other will serve its purpose without any flash.

Hilarious clue for PORNSTAR! [One who's barely acting?], heh. Although I've shied away from these types of entries more and more over the years after receiving solver complaints, the wordplay makes it well worth it.

Along with DUDE BRO and ORGY as a [Bodily function?] … WOOF!

(That's what the kids say these days to express … "that was really bad"? Apparently I've been barking up the wrong tree in my usage.)

Interesting take to combine two well-worn genres in a creative way.
