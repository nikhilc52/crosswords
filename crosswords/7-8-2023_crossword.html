I wanted to make a themeless with a strong 1-Across, so that was my seed entry. My clues appear to be largely intact — although I did have two fewer video game references, two more 90's cultural references, and one more Bong Joon-ho reference. I hope y'all enjoy!

Also — check out my blog for more puzzles, monthly.

Jeff Chen notes:
MADE FOR TV MOVIE is such a fantastic marquee entry. Not only is it a colorful and fun phrase, but it also lends itself to so many ... read more

MADE FOR TV MOVIE is such a fantastic marquee entry. Not only is it a colorful and fun phrase, but it also lends itself to so many great cluing angles. [Film ineligible to get an Oscar] made me wonder what sorts of shenanigans the Academy was up to.

CAESAR DRESSING also worked well. Although CAESAR has been used many times before, including references to the DRESSING, this is the first time we've seen the full entry in an NYT grid. The clue misdirect helps too, [Ranch alternative] possibly pointing to a dude ranch.

NURSING BRA is an excellent entry, and the wordplay around the [Milk duds?] clue is fiendishly clever. So why did that make me uncomfortable? It's possible that I've become oversensitive to certain issues over the years, and that I wouldn't have given things a second thought if the same clue had been presented by a female constructor.

I FEEL SILLY about this in some ways, but that is where I'm at these days. Maybe it's IRRELEVANT. As am I.

The cluing helped the puzzle shine. [Link warning] is quite the sand trap, nudging people to put in FORE instead of NSFW (not safe for work, as in a forwarded link). TIRE SWING literally going out on a limb made me laugh. Repurposing "hunter gatherer" to define a GAME WARDEN was so brilliant that it feels like there's a full crossword theme in there. This dork loved the nerdy {T, H, I, S} mathematical set, too.

I didn't quite FLOAT ON AIR today but I came close to it. Solid gridwork for a 68-worder, with some HIE INT NCO STR to nit-pick.
