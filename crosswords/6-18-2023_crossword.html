Joe DiPietro owns the bar "one-star" in the Chelsea neighborhood of Manhattan. Its website self-deprecatingly calls it "a dingy hole on a deadish street" (but it gets 4.5 stars on Yelp).

This is Joe's 137th crossword for The Times since 1995. He began it after seeing 46-Across on a food package and thinking, hmm. Joe says, "I will never not love coming up with theme ideas and filling grids."

I could use some SAGE ADVICE, considering the number of cooking failures I've had over the past three years. My kids have many a SERVING SUGGESTION, including outspoken ones involving a direct transfer from stovetop to trash bin. Forget about farm-to-table, they're all about pan-to-can.

I laughed at GAME THEORY. I'm fascinated by the subject, and I've never considered it as a hypothesis posited about hunted animals. CORE BELIEF also delighted me, as my insistence on finishing my and Jill's apple cores all the way to the seeds is a source of amusement for her.

BLANKET RECOMMENDATION also worked well because of a huge change in meaning. Not SERVING SUGGESTION as much, which could have been clued to Richard Williams trying to correct Serena's serve.

DISSENTING OPINION was equally flat — perhaps PERSONAL OPINION could have created more laughs by referring to a personal ad, or SECOND OPINION might have been clued to a Swiss watchmaker's thoughts on the exact length of a sixtieth of a minute.

Joe's layout is smart, not unexpected, because he's so accomplished. He packs in so many long Downs by spacing them out perfectly. EGO BOOST and PAUL ANKA aren't hard to stack since they both only intersect one themer, and then THE PLUNGE / NAMETAGS / SHORT CIRCUIT have so much separation that they can be squeezed for every last drop of zinginess. Great stuff. Although there's about AVG glue, featuring such a quantity of worthy bonuses is a great trade-off.

Sage concept that's much fresher than my sage dishes.
