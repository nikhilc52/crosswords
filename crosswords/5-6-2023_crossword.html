I'm pleasantly surprised to be back in the NYT so soon after my debut! This puzzle was submitted in mid-November and accepted in mid-January.

Something I've learned as a constructor is that black square staircases running in the NE-to-SW direction are your best friend. Each white square along the top of the staircase is the last letter of an across entry and a down entry, so using a common terminal letter like S in any of those squares gets you double the bang for your buck.

This was my first real attempt at triple-stacked 15s, and I think I got lucky with how quickly the grid came together. The only section that gave me trouble — and the last to get filled — was the middle region on top of the central staircase (between 19A and 43A). After struggling with it for a while, I tried brainstorming some more possibilities for the 8-letter slot starting with OAT. Happily, OAT-BASED ended up being just what I needed (appropriate enough for a nut-allergic vegan like myself).

Hope you enjoyed this one!

CAROLINA REAPERS / ACTION ADVENTURE / THE NERVE / CRUSADE — reminds me of the "Hot Ones" episode where Pedro Pascal cries from his head!

It's been over a year since we've had two full triple-stacks. There used to be nearly one per month back in Martin Ashwood-Smith's heyday, but they oversaturated the market. These days, I enjoy solving one every now and again, especially when they're anchored by exciting entries like SOPHOMORE SLUMPS and CAROLINA REAPERS. Great clue for the latter, too, the "scythelike" reference helping people unfamiliar with the devilishly hot cultivars.

Full-grid triple-stacks often suffer in their crossings, some truth or dab required to hold everything together. Carter took such care with all of his crossers; so smooth. Maybe soccer and electronics-haters will point to MLS (Major League Soccer) or OHMAGE, but both are reasonable entries. We should all pay ohmage to the top-notch quality.

I wasn't wowed by the modest A MODEST PROPOSAL from almost 300 years ago, or the outdated REEL TO REEL TAPES, reely? The latter also has a high concentration of constructor-friendly letters that make it feel cheap.

Solid work overall, though, a quality example of the well-established genre.
