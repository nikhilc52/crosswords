Sid Sivakumar is an M.D./Ph.D. student at Washington University in St. Louis, training in biomedical engineering and neuroscience. This is his 17th crossword for The Times.

Since the theme material appears mostly in the middle, Sid made the grid's sides — and especially the corners — quite open, giving them a themeless feel. "I was very fortunate they turned out so clean and fresh," Sid says. "I'm usually not so lucky!"

I know that phonetics-based themes can be hit-or-miss because people pronounce words differently. But I enjoy the unusual transformations in these sorts of puzzles. I love to imagine folks saying the answers aloud to themselves as they solve: "Judicious … judicious … ju-DISH-yus …" HEARD THROUGH / THE GRAPEVINE, with its evocative idiomaticity and constructor-friendly 12-letter/12-letter symmetry, struck me as an especially fitting revealer for a phonetic theme, with a visual component to boot.

My guess is that JUDICIOUS, hiding the word "dish," will be the toughest theme answer for most solvers to grok — because it was also the toughest for me to find while constructing. I had already placed the other four Across themers in the grid, intersecting the three grape varieties, but the center of the puzzle was looking far too light on theme material. I searched for words that phonetically encoded other synonyms for "gossip" (buzz? chatter? hearsay?), but none sounded right — and none could intersect CONCORD at the center C. In a last-ditch effort, I wrote some code to mine the CMU Pronouncing Dictionary for other options. JUDICIOUS miraculously turned up to save the day.

Thanks to the Times team for their many excellent improvements to this puzzle (the title was their idea!) and for enabling visual concepts like this one, both digitally and in print.
