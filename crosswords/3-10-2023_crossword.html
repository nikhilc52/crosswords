PREGNANCY PILLOW across the middle provide such support. When Jill had one, I may or may not have stolen it from her in the wee hours a few times. Don't look at me like that — they're comfy!

Great color sprinkled all around, from HOLY TOLEDO to I OWE YOU ONE to SPA WEEKEND to THERE THERE. With this sort of standard themeless layout, those four stacks in the corners have to be filled to their maximum potential. Triplets of snazzy, multi-word phrases like MELLO YELLO / LEGO PERSON / SPA WEEKEND is the opposite of a TRAGIC FLAW.

I remember the first time I encountered ONED. It had to be wrong. Tragic flaw in the puzzle! I've mello yello'ed since then, but I still despise this sort of thing. Same with TENK, though AONE never bothers me. I never said I was consistent!

So much of this is subjective, with some constructors and solvers loving the oddity of only-seen-in-crosswords entries like MANDMS. For some inexplicable reason, I can't stand that one, but PBANDJ is perfectly fine.

Holy Toledo, I wouldn't blame those who decide to UP AND LEAVE my overanalysis.

Incredible clue for BETS. I couldn't figure out [Plays favorites, perhaps] even after getting all four letters from the crosses. That's "plays (the odds-on) favorites (at the sports book)." Wickedly clever. I would have loved more along this wordplay line, instead of the incredibly tough stuff like having to know that Sauron was a LORD.

I know, I have to turn in my dork card now. Tragic flaw, indeed.
