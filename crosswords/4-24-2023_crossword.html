I don't think I've ever said, or will ever say, the phrase "What's poppin?" out loud. Feels a little too. "How do you do, fellow kids?". But, still, a fun concept to build a theme around, and hopefully, it provides a bit of Monday entertainment on par with a pimple-popping video.

Jeff Chen notes:

A couple of years ago, a teenager I've been mentoring asked me WHAT'S POPPIN'. Wondering if he was telling me he wanted to get popcorn or if he was calling me pops, I figured it'd be best to pretend like I knew what he was talking about and say something that sounded equally fresh. Whatever it was, he burst out laughing so hard he nearly choked.

No CHAMPAGNE BOTTLEs for me.

Now that I know what it means, I can appreciate the literal answers to the question. I especially enjoyed the final entry since popping a WHEELIE slightly differs from the others. Although the meaning of "pop" doesn't change much, it was neat to see the range of objects affected.

Except for the PIMPLES. Where's the "unsee" button when you need it?

Thankfully, that was the only blemish in my entire solve. CHAMPAGNE BOTTLE so tidily spans the grid, not forcing any black square placements, and PIMPLES / WHEELIE are easy enough to tuck in most anywhere that there's no need for grid Clearasil. Solid work, incorporating a couple of THREEPIO PARMESAN bonuses while avoiding short acne.

The grid is so clean that I'd love to see what Emily could do if she removed the black square between OPIUM and PANEL to allow for another pair of long bonuses. It's almost always better to offer a smooth Monday fill than a colorful yet crunchy one, but this set of themers should allow for four long Down bonuses.

Inclusion of PIMPLES aside, I admired the creative lateral thinking playing on the fresh phrase (that no one will use anymore, now that I'm saying it).
