Dylan Schiff is a middle school science teacher in Delaware whose extracurricular activities include cooking, bowling, watching game shows, and editing Sporcle quizzes.

This is his third crossword for The Times (and first Sunday). Dylan writes: "My students and fellow faculty members still can't get over the fact that real-life people — let alone someone they know — design crosswords!"

After getting bombarded by a barrage of pop-up ads in late 2021, I thought "How can I recreate this in a crossword puzzle?" What does it say about the abundance and persistence of online marketing that I started viewing it as the potential basis for a crossword theme? I spent a few months brainstorming ideas, put it on hold, and then returned to it with fresh eyes in March 2023. At that point, I expanded my brainstorm to include other computer/online experiences that could be represented literally. The puzzle came together quite readily after that — I submitted the puzzle in April and received an acceptance email in June!

My seventh year of teaching is underway, and I'll be welcoming students later this week. Happy school year 2023-2024!

Day seven of "Gotta JET" (Jeff's expressions of thanks)! There are so many amazing clues in the puzzle today, and that reminds me to give thanks to constructors who dedicate the necessary time to spicing up their solve, especially for the bigger Sunday canvas. It's challenging to keep someone's attention in this age of social media snippets, so amazing clues like [Match making?] sparks a FIRE to keep going.

The NYT editing team deserves a lot of credit as well. They're not only the ones who select what puzzles to run, but they spend a ton of time crafting and honing. I've had the pleasure of getting an inside look at how Will Shortz operates, and he spends so much energy poring over clues, writing new ones, elevating the quality of the solving experience.

Before I sign off, one more note: Finally and most importantly, I appreciate everyone who's read my commentaries over the years. Being able to express my thoughts and opinions has been a tremendous creative outlet for me, and hearing from and interacting with so many people has been a privilege. Thank you to everyone out there, from the bottom of my heart.

Although you won't hear from me regularly through this forum anymore, please do stay in touch.
