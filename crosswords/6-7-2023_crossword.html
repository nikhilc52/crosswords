Thrilled to be back with our third NYT puzzle! We tried hard to make a surfing-themed puzzle work, but decided to pivot to other water puns, and were surprised to find a full set relating to a potential news story. The grid and fill came together nicely after we tried putting a black square in the center of the puzzle. It took a little time to figure out the best clueing angle — but this puzzle by Brian Thomas has always been a favorite, and we took some inspiration from that.

And here is a bit of bonus theme we couldn't fit: "This news report brought to you by ___ " THEATLANTIC. Hope you enjoyed!

Jeff Chen notes:
Hilarious image of a reporter unfamiliar with wordplay, haplessly recounting events around a cruise ship, with viewers wincing the ... read more

Hilarious image of a reporter unfamiliar with wordplay, haplessly recounting events around a cruise ship, with viewers wincing the entire time. BREAKING STORY, cruise ship cracks! LEAKED FOOTAGE shows water seeping in! The crack widens — we're STREAMING LIVE details to you! But wait, an act of God saves them, waves pushing them to safety … stay turned for more CURRENT EVENTS!

Even better if the reporter is Drax the Destroyer.

I don't love 72-word grids in early-week puzzles because they tend to make a solve so much more challenging than the theme. When you're in mid-week territory, though, amping up the grid's difficulty to match that of the theme makes so much sense.

This is the kind of 72-word themed grid I can appreciate. Make your life easier by breaking up CIAOBELLA at the B? Or PLASMA TV at the M? Screw PRACTICAL! If you're going to go big or go home, you have to achieve a ton of go big bonuses with not much go home short fill, and this grid is a success. Along with the aforementioned, BIRD EGGS, IRIS SCAN, Ralph ELLISON, IMMERSE? ET VOILA is right (and PEABRAIN is not)!

More mid-week constructors should try this when working with only four themers — although only if they can keep glue to some minor dabs of AERO FAS URI.

It took me a while to appreciate the cringe-worthy humor, but I appreciated how Jennifer and Victor found a common thread among these common news phrases and turned it into a literalist's nightmare.
