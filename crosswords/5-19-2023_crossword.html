Oh no you didn't, David! My kids have been singing BABY SHARK off and on for years now, and I would usually pay through the nose to knock it out of my head AT ANY COST, POST HASTE. Although, as with everything else, Ted Lasso has provided the answer, Jamie Tartt repurposing the song in such a ridiculous way that it now makes me smile.

The term YANKEE SWAP isn't quite as colorful as white elephant exchange, but it still evokes all sorts of imagery. I'm hopeful that our Mariners somehow can steal Jose Trevino from the Bronx Bombers.

What I'm even more hopeful for: one day having a Dirty Santa party.

Don't (Aaron) Judge me.

Most constructors focus their clever cluing on their feature entries, but it's just as effective in helping regular ol' boring short fill stand out. Usually, you use entries like DYE, HAW, DATE, MILK because they do their job: they don't make a solver hitch. DYE pulls so much weight, though, with a beautiful [What might address a gray area] clue. And I wouldn't hem or HAW about a HAW being found near a hem.

For those unaware of various matchmaking apps that require swiping right and left, a side-swipe might get you a DATE. Finally, I don't have to give a spoiler alert here since there's already one of sorts on MILK cartons — the "best by" DATE!

These four short answers could have been simply fine, but with such brilliant clues, they inject freshness and fun into the solving experience.

Well-constructed themeless, with some strong debut entries and outstanding work in clever cluing.
