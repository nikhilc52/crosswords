There had been some talk recently about Saturday being the new Friday. I suspect that last week's puzzle by KAC and this week's will tamp down such talk for a while.

One standard way of making a puzzle harder is making the clues vague, so they lead you in multiple directions. But it's easy to overdo it, and no one wants to do a puzzle with clues like [Noun] and [Verb]. I like clues that appear vague at first, but then become surprisingly specific as you figure them out. A good example is [They have everyone buzzing] at 35-Across. Initially you might be thinking in several directions, but once you think of buzzing in as in College Bowl, "everyone" becomes a critical part of the definition.

DREAMLIKE state today, to have Waldenesque simplicity in a Walden creation. So often I wade around the murky waters of Byron's ponds, asking myself about any particular entry, IS THIS IT? I did cock my head at WAR BLOGS, HEBREW U and NON PUBLIC, but so much natural beauty in the vowel-heavy JUNEAUITE, along with the explosion of rare letters — eight JQXZs! — gave me a dose of spiritual discovery.

I questioned TOSSUP QUESTIONS at first, but having grown up on "Family Feud," I should have known better. I pondered over [They have everyone buzzing] for an embarrassingly long time, before buzzing in with the number one answer.

The engineer in me delighted over the [Round bits] clue, too. I have so many drill bits of every shape and size that I mentally sorted through every single one before realizing that binary code was in play: 0s are literally round bits, while 1s are straight bits.

I'm not wild about partials like IN GOD or the randomness of EXMET or the -ERS of KNEADERS, but in a 68-word creation, they're reasonable prices to pay. Along with so many rare letters and inspiring clues, I came away with a much calmer sense of Walden than usual.
