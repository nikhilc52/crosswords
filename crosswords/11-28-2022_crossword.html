I've been solving crosswords long and loudly enough that everyone in my life knows about it. In 2019, one of those friends, Pierre-Alexander Low, invited me to see a live crossword show starring Will Shortz and Joel Fagliano in San Francisco. Before the show, Pierre told me a group of Bay Area crossword constructors was having dinner around the corner. Did I want to crash it? (I did). The people I met there were universally friendly, funny, and wicked smart. I'd never thought about making crosswords before. But after that night, I knew I wanted to be part of this community.

Three years, one pandemic, and countless stops-and-starts later...here I am! This is my 3rd published puzzle and my first in the Times. To any other underrepresented folks interested in making crossword puzzles, check out Erik Agard's Crossword Puzzle Collaboration Directory group on Facebook. It's where I met two of my incredible crossword mentors, Ross Trudeau and Paolo Pasco (I met a third, the marvelous Andrea Carla Michaels, through Pierre that night in 2019!).

Jim Horne often advises me to abridge my posts because people get blogorrhea tedium. Hopefully, no one gets HOPPING MAD today because this debut is an excellent "broken words" puzzle.

I appreciated that Chloe incorporated a lot of vivid, LIVID words into her themers. GHOST CHILI is undeniably spicy!

Seven-letter entries are often dull, but not SHEBANG. Given that I've been shifting to a plant-based diet, I even enjoyed RYE SEED. Not as indulgent as RYE BEER, sadly.

Chloe also disguised the theme words well. FUM could have used FEE FI FO FUM, but that would have made FUM stand out. It's so neatly tucked into PARFUM. ING in INGENUES is even better in that it changes the sound of ING between FUMING and INGENUES.

"Broken words" themes often stand out on the strength of

  1. long theme words, or
  2. strings of difficult letters.

FUM/ING is more impressive than IR/ATE on both counts — not only are there tons of entries ending in IR, but you can split it at IRA/TE, too. (SHAKIRA sings La Tortura to protest her exclusion.)

Another aspect that can elevate this type of theme is if it feels impossible to come up with any other possibilities. The only other I turned up (after a solid hour!) was SAYS YOU / TRAGEDY. (Shakira protests me this time, singing HEY YOU.)

A couple of tough entries right off the bat in AMFAR, REI, HAIFA. All are fair game, but AMFAR crossing REI isn't ideal. It'd be a different story if AMFAR or REI were figure-out-able by etymology, like OXFAM (Oxford + famine).

Solid example of the "broken words" genre. The trick didn't feel as much "hop" as "skip," but I did jump at the WELL-AIMED MOON ROVER bonuses.
