Hi everyone! Thrilled to be making my NYT debut today. The inspiration for this puzzle struck me one day while making dinner with my (then) three-year old. I'm happy to report that Friday's are now the traditional 62-Across night in our household. Hope y'all enjoy!

Also, I wanted to take a minute to plug a crossword project I've been working on since this past summer: "lemonade disco". In an effort to support this inspiring community, I basically turned my indie blog into a venue for constructors to be published. I curate, edit, and publish a suite of puzzles each month for the world to solve! Anyone and everyone is welcome to participate! The next suite drops THIS THURSDAY the 17th.

More info can be found at www.taylormadecrosswords.com

Lastly, feel free to reach out! I'm always open to feedback, and I'm always willing to help others curious about the world of crossword construction. You can find me on Twitter @lemonade_disco_

Who doesn't love a PIZZA PARTY? I'll tell you who: two certain kids who refuse to eat anything that a molecule of SAUCE has touched. They will also snub the CRUST if it's too crusty. Or not crusty enough. And they'll only SAY CHEESE to a singular brand of non-dairy, vegan (read: outlandishly expensive) cheese substitute.

Talk about WEAK SAUCE.

What, no toppings on today's pizza?

Oh, right, my kids won't eat toppings on pizza.

I had a laugh/cry with the grid's vibe of TIME BOMBS EXPLODES ANTIMATTER WHAP.

I usually love loads of bonus fill, but I had to weigh its effects today. On one hand, I couldn't easily figure out the theme — were BOMBS and LEAVE thematic? Muddying up a crispy pizza theme isn't great.

On the other hand, I couldn't easily figure out the theme. For such a simple concept, where CRUST SAUCE CHEESE are tough words to disguise, hiding them in plain sight among distracting entries extended my "Name That Theme" game.

So all the Across bonuses gets to the age-old computer science / mechanical engineering question: is a feature or a bug?

Let's go with feature today.

I used to roll my eyes at "directive" clues, but I've come to enjoy them. You'd better believe it! As in believing a DOGMA. Accessible wordplay fun on a Monday is much appreciated.

I'm sure some boomers will say BLECH to GENZER. On an unrelated topic, I might point out that GEN ZER is one letter away from GEEZER.

All in all, a debut with a touch of spice. To which my kids will say BLECH.
