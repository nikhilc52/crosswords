Any else take off their glasses and call their optometrist while solving this puzzle?

Talk about daring! I worked on a puzzle with three adjacent pairs of doubles, and that was hard enough. Double that to six pairs, and you get crazy strings like SSAABBOOKKEE. I can barely look at those twelve letters without thinking I finally need reading glasses.

Some of the theme entries are awkward, like who would ever say QUEEN NOOR ROOMMATES or DOES SAAB BOOKKEEPING, but kookiness is the name of the game with a theme like this. I thought I wouldn't enjoy it after my eyes went unfocused, trying to make sense of SSIINNEEDDOO, but I'm going to remember this one.

I could have done with a couple more bonuses besides PIRATES BOOTY, MAKING A STINK, TRAP DOOR, and a few less ADM ELKE ESE SRTA STS, but all in all, it's a reasonable set of grid trade-offs for a 21x21 puzzle.

I appreciated this one more with each time I re-read the themers. Not all of them hit that strongly, but I have to admire the sheer audacity of the concept. Now I'm wondering what's the largest possible number of doubled letters strung together. If you tossed aside any semblance of the entry making sense, could you achieve ten pairs? Fifteen?

Down the rabbit hole I go ...

(Peter's two scrapped themers: CONDOLEEZZAANNEELLIOT and WALTERREEDDOORREEKED)
