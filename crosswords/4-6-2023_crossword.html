Sam Buttrey notes:
My wife and I have always enjoyed the goofy 1983 movie "Trading Places," and the idea of answers trading places in the grid seemed as ... read more

My wife and I have always enjoyed the goofy 1983 movie "Trading Places," and the idea of answers trading places in the grid seemed as if it might be fun. Originally I thought of characters from the movie — Louis and Billy Ray — doing the trading, but they're obscure, and when I thought of Laurel and Hardy and realized that their names are both regular words I figured I might be on to something. Of course, in a puzzle like this the theme entries don't have to be placed symmetrically, so this gave me a lot of latitude in the choice of grid and where, exactly, to put the themers.

I have now recently crossed (ha!) two items off my bucket list: in addition to getting this puzzle published, I was able to appear on the "Jeopardy!" TV show, first in the inaugural Professors Tournament, and then in the Tournament of Champions, where I finished as "second runner-up," which feels better than "third."

With such juggernauts as Matt Amodio, Amy Schneider, and Mattea Roach looming large in Jeopardy! ToC, seeing the unheralded Sam Buttrey upset the heavily-favored Amodio was awesome. Almost as fun as watching Sam floss!

Great idea to play on disguisable pairs: BILL and TED, LAUREL and HARDY, PENN and TELLER, CHIP and DALE. At first glance, TED might seem like a cheat, but the TED in TED Talks is not named after a guy named Ted, but based on the "convergence of the fields of technology, education, and design."

There aren't many other uber-famous duos that could work this way. Good luck disguising BERT and ERNIE or BONNIE and CLYDE!

We were going to highlight the entries to make them easier to pick out, but that looked a right mess. My anality typically demands some semblance of themer symmetry, but there's something to be said for being a RENEGADE.

It would also have been odd to mix the pairs for the sake of symmetry. LAUREL and TELLER would be one awkward comedy duo.

Throwing symmetry to the wind allowed for freedom in filling, too. Fun to get the symmetrically placed ROAD RAGE and RENEGADE. Sam's a wild man!

I would comment on NINEVEH, but this entry coming from a master of trivia seems just about right.

I'm eager to see what a person of Sam's talents has on his bucket list. Perhaps he'll be up for a [Salad including apples and celery]-type crossword review collaboration …
