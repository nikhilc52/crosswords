A NINE … hit!

N TWO … Bingo!

You sunk my battleship?

My gaming PLOT arc is all bent out of shape.

Using the coordinate system (shown in the grid below), N TWO = column N / row TWO. That square is occupied by the letter P. Following the process with A NINE, D ONE, and K SEVEN, you get P L O T.

P + L + O + T = the PLOT thickens!

PLOT is appropriate because the four letters are indeed PLOTted out on the coordinate plane. I would have loved some additional layer of depth, though — something like PLOT POINTS would have contributed to a stronger a-ha moment.

Great find in STUNTWOMAN, an excellent headliner for this theme. Note how NTWO breaks across STUNT and WOMAN, making it much more interesting than a one-worder like NETWORKING. Editors stress the importance of multi-word themers since they're more colorful as a whole than single-worders.

It would have been great to swap out either CANINE TEETH (Will Shortz would point out that TEETH doesn't contribute to the theme) or INDONESIANS (one-worders). There are many options with TEN, like JET ENGINE, and multi-word options with ONE, like PABLO NERUDA or LEMON EXTRACT.

Not all multi-worders are fantastic, though. One could easily argue that INDONESIANS is way better than the misery of HELL ON EARTH.

The "hidden words" genre is overdone these days, so an extra layer is always appreciated. Although today's didn't grab me as strongly as if there had been something more than simply PLOT at the end, I appreciated the novelty.
