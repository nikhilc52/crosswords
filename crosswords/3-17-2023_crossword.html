This puzzle started with the grid layout — I really like the look of 90-degree rotational symmetry, and I figured the relative separation of the four corners would make it easier to fill each of them independently. The top left stack was the first one I settled on, and I worked my way through the rest of the puzzle from there.

If I were remaking this puzzle today, I'd probably try harder to replace some of the more boring, neutral long entries like LARGE-SCALE, METAL PLATE, and ENDANGERED. I also wish I had been more mindful of space limitations when writing my clues. Still, I'm happy with this as a first effort, especially DRAG MOTHER, BEAUTY REST, and GO COMMANDO.

Lastly, I want to thank my mom and my sister for test-solving. Hope you enjoyed!

Many moons ago, Rich Norris at the LAT told me that GO COMMANDO was too risqué for their solvership. Will Shortz has always been more open to being the head of butt jokes, with the entry previously clued as [Not be underdressed?] and [Not cover one's butt?]. Today's clue rebutts them all with [Join a boxer rebellion?]. I'm all for this race to the bottom! (Sorry.)

Fantastic example of how to do a 70-worder right. The competition is ultra-fierce in this space, so you have to make near-perfect use of your long slots while leaving your short fill flawless. Great color, from JUMP SCARES to BEAUTY REST to PARASAILED to DRAG MOTHER.

Corner stacks aren't difficult, but running long answers through them amps up the difficulty. Doing that for not just two corners but all four makes life interesting. Smart grid layout, deploying so many black squares in the middle to facilitate the melding.

Those long answers intersecting in the middle aren't as colorful as the marquee entries. As Carter mentioned, ENDANGERED is more muted, like EARTH TONES. That's a natural consequence of working with so many constraints, and a fine trade-off to make. Even better is that Carter elevated these entries with clever cluing. [Browns, e.g.] made me think the hapless Johnny Football. And while BLUE WHALES isn't a mind-blowing entry, [Ones calling across the ocean?] employs fun wordplay.

Perfectly fine to have a bit of film marring a beautiful corner, though acknowledging a poor entry like ALGA is better than trying to get cute with the clue. [Bit of film] points longtime solvers toward CEL, and eventually landing on the odd singular wasn't great.

Solid debut, with clearly a ton of work put into both grid and clues.
