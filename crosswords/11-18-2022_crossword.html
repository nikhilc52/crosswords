I hope you enjoy my latest contribution to the New York Times puzzleverse. My favorite entry today has to be the debut of TRASHPANDA. It was a hard one to clue because I think it's an "either you know it or you don't" type of entry.

Nevertheless, I wanted to do something fun with it, so I searched to see if there were other animals with silly nicknames. I found a great one for snake … danger noodle. C'mon, DANGER NOODLE?? That's just awesome. So I clued it as an analogy:

snake : danger noodle :: raccoon : TRASH PANDA.

Sadly, danger noodle was not a big hit with the editors and the clue ended up on the cutting room floor. But my love for danger noodle lives on. I don't typically begin my puzzles with seed entries, but I very well may have to start my next grid with DANGER NOODLE.

TRASH PANDA is so hilarious that it could earn a POW! all on its own. This is the kind of delightful entry that makes me smile with every sighting of a Robyn Weintraub byline.

There weren't as many joyful grid entries as I've come to expect out of a Weintraub production, but that's more of a matter of my sky-high bar for her. Still, phrases like ALL OVER THE MAP and HERE WE GO AGAIN are the foundations of a fantastic CARE PACKAGE.

Even with my unreasonable expectations, Robyn managed to wow me with the sheer quantity of delightful wordplay clues. How many times have I seen the word "toy" played upon for "small dog"? Yet I still fell for it today, because of the mystery created by [Toy from a place that no longer exists]. No longer exists ... Atlantis? Abyssinia? Austria? (I once swore that Austria had dissolved back in the 19th century. I also once confused Wyoming and Wisconsin. Sad, but true.)

Such mastery over the "hidden capital" trick, too. [Superior dwellings, say] obscures the capital S at the beginning of the clue, misdirecting from LAKE HOUSES. Robyn also pulled it off in reverse, with [Spent some time in the Outback, perhaps]. That's a Subaru Outback, not the Austrian Outback, of course.

Most themeless constructors spend way too little time on their cluing — an order of magnitude — and this is a great example of where all of Robyn's cluing efforts elevated the solve to a whole 'nother level. As with most Weintraub puzzles, I could have easily given this one the POW! That was my first instinct after hitting one amazing clue after another, but I figured I'd follow Robyn's example of spreading around the joy.
