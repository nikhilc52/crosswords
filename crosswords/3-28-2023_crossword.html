With most crossword puzzles, the solved written word or words become the end goal. With this puzzle, the written clues and answers are ultimately subordinated to the unwritten, the non-verbal. It is these gestures that, in the end, look down upon the written word (with an eye roll), reminding us that a simple gesture can communicate so much more efficiently. Yes, the written words are there, but only to serve the non-verbal, the unwritten. We found this idea fun and even funny. After a few go-arounds with the editorial team, we got a thumbs up on a grid and fill that hopefully wouldn't cause solvers too much head scratching.

SHOOT 'EM UP LOW-CUT PIEHOLE IN THE BUFF? I don't know what game we're playing, but I want in!

I love colloquial phrases in crosswords. I say [Woo-hoo!] whenever I see entries like PUH-LEASE.

(Although, I like it a little less every time my daughter says it to me, with an EYE ROLL so big we have to take her to the optometrist.)

EYE ROLL, FACE PALM, and FIST PUMP are awesome phrases, but I was somewhere between [Har-har-har] and [D'oh!] upon filling in BROW WIPE and KNEE SLAP. KNEE SLAP ...

(Sorry, short pause while I look for Tess's eyeballs.)

Five short themers leave so much room for freestyling in fill, especially when your middle entry is only seven letters long. Notice how you can place a black square on either side of EYE ROLL and continue to grid with little to no reduction in flexibility.

Man, did Daniel and Jay go hog wild! Not only the aforementioned, but CHEATS IDIOT DEAD RUSSIA BADASS A DUEL MAFIA … some more uptight solvers might say ["You know better than ___"], but I enjoyed my experience. In the same way a Tarantino movie janks up my world perspective.

I wish the first two entries had been as strong as the last three, but all the colloquial clues and badassery in the fill still made for a wild ride.
