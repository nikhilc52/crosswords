After dabbling in themeless construction mostly at AVCX+ and with Rafa at Universal, I'm thrilled to have my first themeless puzzle in The Times. I'm a sucker for a good spanner, and since I am actually a scientist, 34-Across struck me as the perfect seed entry. My favorite clue is at 1-Across; I'd been waiting to use it for a while and was glad to use it to open this puzzle. I hope you enjoy!

Jeff Chen notes:
Day six of "Gotta JET" (Jeff's expressions of thanks)! I love it when a constructor features a zingy phrase, and it's especially ... read more

Day six of "Gotta JET" (Jeff's expressions of thanks)! I love it when a constructor features a zingy phrase, and it's especially notable when it's related to that person. Rebecca is most definitely a scientist, so I'M NOT A SCIENTIST (but …) gave me a kick.

It also gave me the perfect opportunity to say dammit, Jim Horne, I'm not a blogger! Oh, wait. Maybe I am. Huge thanks to Jim, who's pushed me to develop my voice over the past decade. I've been a writer for 15 years now, and I credit Jim for his gentle but firm nudges to step outside my comfort zone.

More importantly, I appreciate his friendship over the years, especially important throughout those dark pandemic years. Our weekly calls to discuss the latest batch of puzzles was such a highlight, an event I could depend upon for regular connection, insight, and laughs.

Never mind that it was always Jim bursting into cackles at my idiocy!

Now in my 50s, making new friends is rarer than ever. This XWord Info journey has been full of ups and some big downs, but it's all been worth it, largely because it's allowed me to take part in a grand partnership journey with a great guy.
