I'm pleased to have this puzzle on July 4. I hope it enhances your 4th-of-July cookouts.

I got the idea for this puzzle when it seemed like pickleball was everywhere all of a sudden. As a pickle afficionado (I've even made pickles a few times), I wanted to take advantage of that. It was good fortune that the Knives Out sequel came out around the same time.

My favorite clue was the one for 3-Down.

This puzzle was accepted as a probable Monday. It's being published on Tuesday because of its holiday-friendly theme, which puts me just one daunting day away from the cycle … anyone want to collaborate on a Saturday?

Hardly the PLAINEST of themes, with all the trimmings of a BURGER — so neatly lined up in the middle! It's like an exploded view drawing of a TOMATO, ONION, PICKLE, and CHEESE that you might see in a McDonald's manual. (If you haven't seen The Founder yet, Michael Keaton puts some amazing special sauce into the role.)

It's nearly impossible to disguise what's going on, given that food is food is food. GLASS ONION is an excellent choice, though, delaying the aha moment a little. Much better than picking something like GREEN ONION, which not only has no misdirection but would also have confused the recipe. Ray Kroc would have snapped his jaws at that.

I paused after solving. Why not use a more complete set of ingredients, like in a PBJ a few weeks ago? No buns, no meat ... just the fixings? It's like Barb designed this for my hamburger-snubbing kids (who won't eat anything but the CHEESE. And heaven help us if that dares to be anything but the crazy-expensive brand of non-dairy cheese they're used to.)

It's not a new concept to weave recipes into crossword puzzles, but stacking the four elements almost directly over each other (my anality forces me to point out that they could have been slightly more aligned by using a 16-wide grid) is a cool touch.
