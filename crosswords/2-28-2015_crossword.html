My original version of this puzzle had two seeds: NO EASY DAY at 1-Across and PHOTOBOMB. I had just finished reading the NYT best seller "No Easy Day" (about the mission that killed Bin Laden) which had been recently published. So, I was inspired to use that as my seed entry.

However, Will had a problem with something else in the NW and in the SE and asked me to rework those corners. I also had LOLZ (clued as "Online guffaws") at 23-Down in the accepted revision, which to my disappointment was edited out in the published version and replaced with the less-scrabbly TOLD.

Barry's mellifluous last name usually lulls me into anticipating a smooth solve. How could someone named Silk create a puzzle that's so rough on my brain? Perhaps my PONS is the problem.

I was surprised to see PANAMA HAT and PAN-AM in the same grid. I mistakenly assumed that PANAMA derived from Pan-America-something, but it actually has indigenous roots, referring to the land's abundance of fish, trees, and/or butterflies.

The PHOTOBOMB/BLURAY crossing introduces two debut words and conjures one of the intertubes' happiest memes. (Fine, yes, a BLURAY is different from a stingray, but there's enough blue in the photo to make it work.)

I am neither sailor nor rear admiral, so ABAFT was new to me. It sounds good and pirate-y, and there's plenty of time to practice using it before International Talk Like A Pirate Day (September 19th).

TITFER is good old Cockney rhyming slang, my absolute favorite linguistic WTF. Jerry Seinfeld's old AmEx commercial was the first time I encountered it, and it's used throughout the Ocean's Eleven franchise by Basher, played by Don Cheadle. It's also why Jeff and I both like to refer to Jim Horne as "Flam Borg."
