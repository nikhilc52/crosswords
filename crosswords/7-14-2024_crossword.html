I have a tough time when my mind doesn't have enough to chew on. It's such a blessing when a rough idea pops into my head, compelling me to dig in. (To clarify, a blessing for me, not for my poor wife, who has to deal with my constantly glazed-over eyes.) The rough idea for this puzzle came to me when I saw headlines about ISSUES around Dr. SEUSS — that was a good start, but so many palindrome puzzles have been made that I wanted some extra layer to help elevate this.

Aha! What if I strategically placed black squares throughout the grid, so that I could clue the themers normally, and then clue them a second time — backward! For instance, 22-Across would be [Rock group clashes over album art?], and then I'd add in the tricky 24-Across, cluing it as [tra mubla revo sehsalc puorg kcoR?]. That seemed interesting enough for me to dive in, using a combination of manual letter-doodling into pages of graph paper, and spending dozens of hours of scouring computer-generated search strings.

Alas, Joel and the team thought the palindromes were solid enough that we didn't need those backward clues, and I realized too late that adding seven extra clues would make the list of clues overflow The Times Magazine's space limitations. It's a bummer that I didn't think this through carefully enough and figure out a way to conserve more cluing space, but it's something I'll take away for the future.

Now, will someone please write me with something interesting to occupy my stupid brain?
