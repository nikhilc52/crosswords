Jeff Chen notes:
I appreciate that even though Ryan is already one of the best Saturday themeless constructors in the crossworld, he continues to push ... read more

I appreciate that even though Ryan is already one of the best Saturday themeless constructors in the crossworld, he continues to push his own limits with challenging grid patterns. Today's uses one of his football-ish middle shapes but tilted upward. It's amazing how many long entries he jam-packed into the middle: six long Acrosses woven through seven long Downs!

Because the middle is so open, with excellent solving feng shui, it's difficult to integrate much more into the grid once you fix your middle in place. Yet Ryan managed to squeeze yet more juice out of his upper left and lower-right corners, with BARBELL and I SEE NOW / MANACLE / STATE ID jazzing up the joint.

The cluing also enhancing my solve: a TADPOLE growing up to croak. AYE said, "in passing" (a parliamentary measure). OIL HEAT for a "house-warming". And best for last, this board gamer loved the misdirect in "cut the cards," as in "forget about the (cue) cards" to AD LIB.

Each one of these clues is designed to make me, as a solver, feel smart. Saturdays often come with cluing that brags about how much smarter the constructor is than me (not a high bar), and I appreciate that Ryan took care to deliver wicked wordplay while making me feel happily smug.

I have an incredibly high bar for Ryan, and entries like HORSEFLESH and THUNDERS don't do as much for me as JET SETTERS and SOLO ARTISTS, which are real SPITFIRES. Even after solving so much of his work, though, I still am filled with a sense of awe, seeing a grid with a big, open middle like today's.
